# Through the looking-glass, found.

Here is a brief motivation for building this site.

I'd like to think we have presets: born with things to do, problems to fix, lives to live. Reality, however, speaks a different language. As Clifford Geertz once said in his _Interpretation of Cultures_:

> Man is an animal suspended in webs of significance he himself has spun.

So we move on, inventing tools that extend our webs, and the realm of men is nothing but the realm of great significance.

But when the web grows so large, one often has the danger of letting its own creation define itself.

Then we are lost.

We can rediscover ourselves once more by building "mirrors". When looking into them, we suddenly discover that the information we generate no longer restricts us: if the world in the mirror is fake, why does it contain the same content? The only explaination is that we are more than the information reflected.

Game is such a device. If a person is defined by being a businessman, how come he could play as a warrior? More abstractly, how can you still know there is a "world", when it's rendered by little squares on a computer? There must be something higher above that enables all of these. It's not God, it's who we really are.

That's why I enjoy building games and talking about them. It's an especially urgent craft for today's world. When technology reversely determines what we can do and think, game could very well be the last resort of our significance and creativity. To quote Chaplin on this:

> We have developed speed, but we have shut ourselves in. Machinery that gives abundance has left us in want. Our knowledge has made us cynical; our cleverness, hard and unkind. We think too much and feel too little.<br>...<br>machine men with machine minds and machine hearts!

I hope to share my thoughts and methodologies on crafting the experience of play through video games. I wish you can share this crude little web of mine, and then we shall build greater stuff together.
